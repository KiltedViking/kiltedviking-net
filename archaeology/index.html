Eskilstuna

What can I say, Eskilstuna is a manufacturing town... But there are still some buildings of interest.

Most of the city centre is from the 19th or 20th century, even if the town dates back before the 11th century. Some older wooden house, mostly smithies and other craftsmen's houses, where moved to the northwest of the city centre, an area called Rademacher smithies (Rademachersmedjorna). Some of the smithies are still working making, amoung other things, pocket knives, which Eskilstuna is known for. Conveniently some old wooden houses burnt down to make way for the first ugly, modern building for the university (which I worked in). But nice enough they restored old factory buildings for the next two blocks of the university.

The town hall is interesting (and old) as is the old Tunafors chuch (to the east of the square by the river). South along the river is the old factory, from the time when even factory buildings had an impressive facade. Across the river (the east side) is the old town with many old and interesting buildings. Further east is the old king's barn with an old bronze age fort on top of a rock.

South of the railway was, what I have seen from pictures and photos, a quaint neighbourhood, only to be torn down a replaced with concrete housing blocks (as part of the "million program "). To their defence I gather the state of the old houses were poorly.

Unfortunatly (?) most monasteries where torn down in the 16th century (for other buildings), as was Eskilstuna's to build a castle, which burnt down (also in the 16th century). We are left with some foundations and street names showing where both buildings where.

My house

The last 7 years (2006) I have lived in a typical Swedish house from the 1940s (another "million program"). The craftsmenship is crappy and the building material is not top notch, but it's good wood (not like today's where it rots in a few years if mishandled). If I drop a marble I know where to look for it (in one of house's corners). :-)

Malmköping

Malmköping is a small quaint town to the south of Eskilstuna. It is an old regimental town (cavalry) and has a military vehicle museum (Swedish vehicles). It has a tram-car museum, even though the town never has been big enough for trams. The trams run on the old railway line (which nowadays run east of Malmköping).

Malmköping is part of Flen's municipality (see Flen for more information in English).

Strängnäs

Strängnäs is a small quaint town to the east of Eskilstuna. It has been a Christian centre for a long time and some bishop (or rather) is placed there. It is also an old regimental town (armour), down-sized in recent years.
